Examples of Applications



Cosmetics: Measuring Quality of Fragrance Oils

High value Perfume oils are a profitable target for counterfeiters. Lower quality counterfeits are often sold at lower cost on various platforms. A test with spectrometer measuring light absorbance can determine if the spectral signature of perfume really matches the proprietary reference composition set by the company. Genuine Perfume oils have distinctive peaks at specific wavelengths which can differ considerably in intensity and wavelength from adulterated oils. This simple method can distinguish pure perfume oils from counterfeit products.



Food and Beverage: Quality Assurance of Wines

Spectral signature of absorbed light can help determine the quality and age of a wine. Winemakers use spectrometers to measure the properties of their wine and prevent potential problems. Chemical composition has a major effect on the notes and tone of the final product, spectrometers can objectively measure the signature of various chemicals in wine and provide an feedback for winemakers.



Industrial : End point detection in Semiconductor processing

In Semiconductor Industry, during Plasma etching process, reactant products related to removal of etched layer produce characteristic spectral lines in the plasma glow, which are used to determine status or progress of the plasma etching process. For example, during SiO2 etch by CHF3 and Ar, prominent spectral lines are produced by CO, CO2, OH, SiF2 and SiO. By monitoring presence of these lines we can monitor progress of the process.
